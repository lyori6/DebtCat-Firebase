Struggling with medical debt in California? Starting January 1, 2025, a new law (SB 1061) will eliminate medical debt from credit reports, offering financial relief and potentially boosting your credit score.

Struggling with medical debt in California? We've got some game-changing news that could boost your credit score. Starting January 1, 2025, medical debt will vanish from your credit report thanks to a new state law (SB 1061). Here's what you need to know to protect your rights.

Key Changes Coming Your Way

  • Medical debt can't appear on your credit reports
  • Lenders can't use medical debt against you in credit decisions
  • Healthcare providers must inform you about these protections
  • These rules apply to hospital bills, ambulance services, and skilled nursing care

What This Means for Your Wallet

Did you know that 38% of Californians carry medical debt? If you're one of them, this law is your financial fresh start. Here's why it matters:

  • Your credit score won't take a hit from medical bills
  • Easier approval for apartments and loans
  • Better chances when job hunting
  • Protection from medical debt harassment

Know Your Rights

  • Any medical debt reported after January 1, 2025, is automatically void
  • Creditors can't use your medical debt in credit decisions
  • You can take legal action if providers violate these rules
  • This applies to most medical services (except elective cosmetic procedures)

Take Action Now

  1. Check your credit report for medical debt (use Credit Karma or AnnualCreditReport.com)
  2. Document any medical debts you currently have
  3. Save this article for reference when the law takes effect

Remember: While this law offers powerful protection, don't ignore medical bills completely. They're still valid debts - they just can't hurt your credit score anymore.
